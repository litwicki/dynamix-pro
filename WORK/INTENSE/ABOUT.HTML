It started the first day I got Adobe Photoshop™ whenever that was, and hasn't ended since.  It was around Christmas time of '98 I believe, but that doesn't make a difference.  I started looking at sites for tutorials so I could do some freaky tweaking with my graphics and do stuff like Mark Monciardini could. Then I came across Phong, and have since then vowed to do my best to not become anything remotely close to him.  

No "god@dynamixpro.com" or "My Desktop."  Who the hell wants to see a screenshot of my desktop?  If you are that desperate to be just like Phong, or anyone for that matter, then you deserve to be smacked upside the head with a long wet spaghetti noodle. It's disgusting how many minions Phong has, and how little respect he gives to them.  I'll never be as popular as Phong as an artist, regardless of skill of any kind.  Mark (DesignsByMark) on the other hand is quite the opposite of Phong, in my perspective.  Quite a nice gentlemen, and he deserves a handshake for what he's done for the community, (of which I take no part in) as well as digital artists all over the world. 

I'm in this for fun, not to be an internet "stud."  I like computers, I like art, so I do computer art, that simple.  If you like it, you'll be back more than once, and if you don't, then you'll go back to wherever you prefer to visit daily.  You won't ever see a "coming soon" or "under construction" message anywhere on the site, and if you do, don't bother coming back because the site will have been deleted by then.  That simple.  Take it, or leave it?  We'll find out, won't we?
